Analyzing customer browsing behavior and predicting purchase probability
during peak shopping months using machine learning.

---

## 📌 Overview

Online shopping decisions rely heavily on how consumers engage with store
content. This project analyzes a dataset of online shoppers' purchasing
intentions gathered over a full year, with a focused lens on **November
and December** — the busiest months for retail.

The goal is to support a startup's marketing team in gauging potential
campaign success by estimating purchase probability across two key
customer segments.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Explore and analyze customer browsing behavior in `November` and `December`
- Identify two target customer groups: **low purchase rate** and **returning customers**
- Estimate the probability that customers in these groups will make a
  purchase in an upcoming marketing campaign
- Generate actionable insights to inform next year's sales strategy

---

## 🗂️ Dataset

The dataset contains online shopper session records collected over 12
months, including browsing behavior, page interaction metrics, and
purchase outcomes.

Key features include: session duration, page values, bounce rates,
visitor type, and the binary revenue target variable.

---

## 🔍 Methodology

1. Filter and segment data by month (`Nov`, `Dec`)
2. Identify low purchase rate customers via behavioral thresholds
3. Isolate the returning customer cohort using visitor type flags
4. Apply probabilistic modeling to estimate purchase likelihood per group
